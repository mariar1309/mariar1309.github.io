Images of the Russian Empire

Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii photo collection

CS 180 Fall 25 - Maria Rufova

Introduction

In 1907, Russian chemist Sergey Prokudin-Gorsky traveled the Russian Empire taking the world's first color images. He used a black and white camera and took sets of 3 images, each through a blue, green, and red colored filter. The results are the Prokudin-Gorskii Collection.

The goal of this project is to take digitized versions of his collection from the Library of Congress and reconstruct the color images by automatically aligning each image in the collection. The output will be 3-channel color images showing the original subject in color.

Examples of original glass plate images showing the three color channels stacked vertically.

Approach

To align the images, the image is first cut into thirds to separate the blue, green, and red channels. The green and red channels are then aligned to the blue channel
