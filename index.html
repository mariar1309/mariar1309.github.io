About

Maria Rufova is a senior studying Computer Science and Electrical Engineering at UC Berkeley, graduating May 2026. Her interests span machine learning, systems, and human-AI interaction.

She has interned twice at Microsoft — most recently on the Copilot Platform, where she built a compliance-focused Declarative Agent for enterprise use. She is also a research assistant at the Gopnik Cognitive Development and Learning Lab, studying how children and AI agents approach open-ended exploration, with work published at NeurIPS 2023. As a member of GamesCrafters, she works on strongly solving abstract strategy games in C.

Outside of work, she is involved in CS Scholars, the Society of Women Engineers, and the Association of Women in EE&CS.
